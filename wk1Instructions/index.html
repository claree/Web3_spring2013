How to make a deliciously strong red Sangria

Once February hits, I’m always just aching for summer. Red Sangria is a great way to keep warm, but also is reminiscent of warm summer nights spent wining and dining outdoors. Here, I will share with you step-by-step instructions to make a strong and refreshing sangria, a recipe I used in my past dabbles in the sangria business.

    You will need:

  • 2 750 ml red wine: Shiraz, Pinot, Cabernet...really whatever you prefer/have in the house/ is on sale. (I tend not to use Merlot, as the goal is not to take a nap.)

  • 2/3 cup triple sec

  • 2/3 to 3/4 cup brandy (ok, I’m lying here...start with 1/3 and adjust to your taste and tolerance

  • 5 clementines (oranges are so predictable)

  • 3 persimmons---you wll be pleasantly surprised!

  • 1 pomegranate. yup, its amazing

  • 1 or 2 lemons (this depends on your own love of citrus)

  • 1 bottle of soda water

  • and a big carafe to put this in

  • cutting board, knife, large stirring spoon, all also necessary

Lets make some magic!

First, Lets start with the fruit.


Make sure the persimmons have been washed. Then cut them up into small cubes: 1/4 to 1/2 inch. Just eye it, don't stress.


Then peel all of the clementines.


Now I know what your thinking--the dreaded pomegranate--but not to worry, I have a great tip for these guys:


Fill a bowl with water.

Then cut the pomegranite into fourths.



Take one fourth and while is is submerged in the bowl of water, loosen all the seeds out.

The seeds will sink but the white peel will float, and the juice will stay in the water and off your white shir!

Repeat with all 4 segments. Then scoop the floating white peel our with your hand. Last, pour the bowl of pomegranate seeds and water into a strainer.

Voila.


Take the pomegranate seeds, and with the back of a spoon, crush them a bit some some of the juices come out. Not too much, you don't want them all to be smushy.


Pour the pomegranate seeds into the carafe.


Then add the rest of the fruit.


Next, open both bottles of wine.



Pour both bottles of wine into the container over the fruit.


Then add 2/3 cup of brandy.



Stir it up.


Taste test.


Here's where I usually add some fresh lemon juice and a bit more brandy.


Each batch will taste differnt based on the sweetness of the fruit and the choosen wines, so use your best judgement.


Cover the container and put in the refrigerator to set overnight.


When serving, use a soup laddle to ensure getting some fruit in each glass. Leave a little space and top each cocktail off with soda water. (or do what I do, and add soda water first, then sangria. Not stirring necessary.)


Oh yeah, add some ice.


Cheers!
